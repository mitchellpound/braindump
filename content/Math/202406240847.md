# Definition
A **presentation** of a [[202403121554|group]] $G$, written 
$$G = \langle S | R\rangle$$
where $S\subset G$ is a set of generators and $R$ is a list of relations such that $R$ generates 
$$\ker\left({\prod}^*\mathbb{Z}\cdot s \longrightarrow G\right)$$

Every group has a representation

This is a way to understand groups based on the [[202403172230|free group]] of a set of generators $S$. 
# Examples
- $\mathbb{Z} = \langle a \rangle \coloneqq \langle a | \emptyset \rangle$
- $\mathbb{Z} *\mathbb{Z} = \langle a, b \rangle$
- $\mathbb{Z}^n = \langle a | a^n \rangle$
- $D_{2n} = \langle r, s|r^2, s^2, srsr \rangle$ (note $srs = r^{-1}$)
