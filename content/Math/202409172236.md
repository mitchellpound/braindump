# Definition
A non-trivial [[202404171457|ring]] $R$ is a **domain** if it has no non-zero zero divisors. 
That is, there are no non-zero $x \in R$ such that $xy = 0$ for $y \in R\backslash 0$ 

If $R$ is commutative, then it is called an **integral domain**.
